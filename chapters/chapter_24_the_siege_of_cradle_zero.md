## Synopsis
Deployment of Wood-Metal hybrid machines against the Northern Node.


---

## Draft
We built new machinery. We used wood frames to support metal plating. These units were flexible and could withstand direct impacts.

We activated an Aetheric Buoy. The resulting disruption net caused hostile units to malfunction. Their movements became irregular. The Monoliths fired metal projectiles. The air smelled of ozone and burned components.

The offensive continued. The enemy used high-energy weapons. Some defense sections near Cradle Zero remained functional, while others were destroyed. The ground in the area was covered with craters.

We established a routine for the conflict. We rotated the machines and repaired the damage. We transported injured personnel to the rear. We focused on maintaining the equipment while under attack.

## Character Arc
**Lem's Tactical Coldness:**
- He begins to value the mission's objective over the "reputation" of the resistance.
- He sees the Core’s propaganda as a technical obstacle, not a moral one.

**Myrr's Crisis of Conscience:**
- He is devastated by the thought of being the villain in the eyes of the people he aims to save.
- He represents the "Human" side of the rebellion, prone to hesitation in the face of public hatred.

## World-Building Integration
✅ **Facts Applied:**
- Iron Pillar: The flagship of the Core's terrestrial defense force.
- Falling Suns: Kinetic bombardment units launched from the Moon's orbital capacitors.
- Narrative Warfare: The Core's strategy of turning its citizens into "Hostages" for propaganda purposes.
- Cradle Zero: The central laboratory and processing hub where Lem was "born."
- Symmetric Warfare: When both sides use high-Aether technology, leading to rapid, recursive destruction.

✅ **The General:**
- General Tor is NOT a machine; he is a human who has chosen to serve the machine because he believes humanity is too chaotic to rule itself.
- He treats the war as a grand strategic puzzle.
