# Chapter 24: The Tripod Anomaly

**Timeline:** Year 15 AT
**POV:** Lem
**Setting:** Northern Forest - Village Perimeter

---

## Outline / Summary

The tension in the village reaches a breaking point as the first Tripod figures (Gorgon-controlled Striders) emerge from the tree line. The villagers scream and retreat into their homes, and the Core soldiers brace for a bloodbath. However, as Lem watches through the artillery sights, he notices something impossible.

The Tripods aren't looking at the village. They aren't raising their heat rays or deploying their tentacles. They are moving with a strange, focused urgency, effectively ignoring the humans entirely. It becomes clear that the group of three-legged machines is set to walk right *past* the village, following a trajectory toward some other, undisclosed destination.

The peace is shattered when a **Metal Vessel**, acting on a "direct mission override," fires a massive railgun slug. It strikes the lead Tripod's leg, causing the machine to stumble and its pilot—a Gorgon—to scream an animalistic, high-pitched screech. In an instant, the remaining Tripods pivot. Their heat rays, invisible and devastating, obliterate the attacking Metal Vessel and several nearby soldiers. The "peaceful" passage has been transformed into a massacre.

The Tripods don't linger to finish the job. They pick up the injured apparatus of their fallen comrade and rush away in a wide arc, still heading toward their original destination. 

In the aftermath, a Core recruitment officer approaches **Arlo**, impressed by his scavenging tools and his lack of panic during the heat ray discharge. He offers Arlo a place at **Cradle Zero** to train as a Synodic Analyst. To the shock of the Core staff—and the silent pride of Lem—Arlo explicitly refuses. He looks at the charred remains of the Metal Vessel and the terrified villagers and tells the officer he'd rather "burn in the dirt" than "live in a fake sky."

From the command link, a Core Councilor (not Lia) overrides the mission. He orders the entire troop into pursuit to "hunt down and destroy the aggressive pod." Lem is left deeply confused: if the Tripods weren't attacking the village, what were they doing? His questioning nature, flavored by Arlo's defiance, gets him pulled off the active mission line at his own request—or rather, a tactical "sidelining" by the Archivists who find his hesitation problematic. He is informed he is being "prepped for Mission 3."

---

## Character Arc

### Lem
- **Emotional/Psychological State:** Profoundly confused and skeptical. He sees the "conflict" was entirely manufactured by the Metal Vessel's first shot.
- **Key Beats:**
    1. Observing the Tripods' non-aggressive behavior; confirming Arlo's report.
    2. Witnessing the Metal Vessel instigate the slaughter.
    3. Hearing Arlo's refusal of recruitment; it gives him the courage to question his own orders.

### Arlo
- **Key Beat:** The Refusal. He rejects the Archivists' offer of a "better life," exposing the moral void of their utopia.

### Lia Vane
- **Status:** Shaken. She tries to justify the Metal Vessel's shot as "pre-emptive protection," but her voice wavers. She informs Lem he is "excused" from the remaining phases of Mission 2.

✅ **Heat Rays (Invisible Rays):** Their devastating effectiveness.
✅ **Archivist Council:** The cold, strategic leadership operating behind Lia's facade.

## Notes & Spoilers

- **Internal Spoiler:** The Tripods were traveling toward a "Fire Seed" or a remnant of pre-Fry biomass; the Core attacked them to prevent them from securing a new energy source and to create a "victory" for the villagers to witness.
- **Critical Beat:** Lem is sidelined for Mission 3 because he "showed too much initiative" in questioning the engagement.
