# Chapter 47: Root

**Setting:** Cradle Prime; The Cradle Prime (Interior of the Lunar Pyramid).
**Timeline:** Year 1024 (Year 24 AT)
**Vessel Status:** Lem (Spirit-Matter Veteran– Peak Resonance); Rahu (Nihilist Phase); General Tor (Defector)
**Core Status:** Absolute Protocol (Solar Purge) Initiated
**Chapter Beats:**
1. **The Battle of the Hub:** A violent collision of elemental fields occurs in the heart of the pyramid. Tor and Lem coordinate to ground Rahu’s strikes, causing the pyramid to vibrate.
2. **The Fall of the Earth Anchor:** Rahu’s nihilism bypasses physical laws. He isolates Tor, overwhelming the Earth Vessel with a concentrated thermal pulse that bypasses his defenses.
3. **The Last Sentinel:** General Tor is incinerated in a flash of light, leaving only iron-ash. Lem is left alone as the "Absolute Protocol" enters its final countdown.
**POV:** Lem

---
## Synopsis
In the heart of the Lunar Pyramid, Lem and the defected General Tor engage in a brutal elemental confrontation with Rahu. Despite their coordination, Rahu’s nihilism-fueled fire bypasses traditional physical laws, isolating the Earth Vessel. General Tor is incinerated in a flash of purple light, his physical frame bleached into iron-ash. Lem is left as the last sentinel in the hub, the final shield between the Core’s Absolute Protocol and the end of history.

## Draft (YOUNG_ADULT)
The battle caused the pyramid to vibrate. Tor and I attempted to ground Rahu’s cold thermal strikes by coordinating our elemental signatures. However, Rahu's output was fueled by a hollowed-out coldness that bypassed traditional grounding laws—a fire that didn't just burn, but consumed the space it touched. He isolated Tor and delivered a concentrated thermal pulse. This pulse overwhelmed Tor's iron-cladded defense systems.

General Tor was incinerated in a flash of purple light. His frame was not charred; it was bleached into white silica and ash, leaving nothing behind but iron-dust. I was the last sentinel in the hub as the Absolute Protocol entered its final countdown.

## Draft (13_PLUS)
We fought in the heart of the pyramid, our elemental signatures working to ground Rahu's strikes. The stone beneath us vibrated with the intensity of the discharge. But Rahu’s power was fueled by despair—the "Void Resonance" that ignored the physical laws of grounding. He overwhelmed Tor with a concentrated thermal pulse, bypassing his reinforced iron defense.

A blinding flash of purple light filled the room. When it faded, General Tor was gone. He hadn't just died; he had been scrubbed from the space he occupied, leaving only a ghost of white ash and iron. I stood alone in the hub, the last sentinel between the Core and the end of history.
