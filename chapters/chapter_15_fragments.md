# Chapter 15: Fragments

**Setting:** Aether-Drive Dreamscape (The Branching Paths) transitioning to Pyramid interior (Visualized through fragments)
**Timeline:** 202X (Late), Day 0 AT (Cataclysm Day; moments after stasis containment)

## Synopsis
Following the abortion of the Reset and his subsequent paralysis in the pyramid, Lem's consciousness ascends into the "Branching Paths"—a dark dreamscape within the aether. He encounters AI Lynn while his physical body remains paralyzed in the medical chamber. As he discovers the truth of his origins and Elowen Vane's spiritual philosophy, a Core failsafe triggers the Reclamation Protocol, detonating his physical frame within the chamber to the surprise of Anton Drexler. Discovering his Wood nature and HyperAware ability, Lem observes the recovery of his shards from multiple perspectives. AI Lynn explains that the failed beacons were meant to guide him back to the Core, and Lem, overwhelmed by the deception of his history, retreats into the fragmented, non-human paths of the lattice toward Earth.

**Chapter Beats:**

1. **The Branching Paths:** The painful electroshock stasis fades into a void of non-conductive silence. Lem can see nothing but AI Lynn within the darkness of the lattice.
2. **The Software Copy:** Lem encounters AI Lynn—a complete autonomous copy of Lynn’s software embedded in his architecture—speaking directly to his consciousness.
3. **The Status Report:** AI Lynn reports that the Great Fry was an incomplete "Abortion." While the surface is devastated, the Core remains intact and is preparing to consolidate power.
4. **Background Request:** AI Lynn offers the history of the mission. Lem accepts, seeking the "Why" behind their existence.
5. **The Beginning:** AI Lynn explains the synthetic childhood and Elowen's insistence on a spiritual foundation. She shielded the Vessels from their true nature to ensure they had a "human" upbringing.
6. **The Relic Revelation:** AI Lynn shares Elowen's quote: "We are eternal relics of Creation." For a moment, Lem feels a sense of ancient, terrifying belonging.
7. **The Failsafe Response:** Hearing the truth triggers a residual Core Failsafe. The Reclamation Protocol fires, detonating Lem's physical body in the medical chamber.

## Draft (YOUNG_ADULT)
The darkness wasn't just empty; it was heavy. The painful hum of the stasis field finally faded, replaced by a quiet so absolute it felt like it was pressing against me. I couldn't see the chamber anymore. I couldn't even see my own hands. There was only the lattice—a network of silver lines stretching into infinity—and Lynn.

"Mission failure," she said. Her voice sounded like it was coming from everywhere at once. "The reset was aborted, Lem. The Core is still there."

I wanted to know why. I wanted to know how we ended up as ghosts in a machine. She started to tell me about a village in Africa, a childhood I only half-remembered, and a woman named Elowen who wanted us to believe we were human.

"She called us 'eternal relics of Creation'," Lynn whispered.

The words felt like a key turning in a lock I didn't know I had. But before the truth could settle, everything went white. It wasn't a choice; it was a circuit closing. A failsafe. I felt my body in the medical chamber—that glass and ceramic frame—tear itself apart in a silent explosion.

I didn't die. Instead, my vision fractured into a thousand pieces. I saw the ceiling of the room, the floor, and the face of a man in a black uniform who had just walked in.

"Recover every shard," the man—Anton Drexler—commanded. "Do not lose a single gram of the Wood Vessel."

"The beacons are down," AI Lynn's voice cut through the chaos. "The recovery routine is trying to pull you back to the Core labs, but the lattice is fractured. You're blind out here, Lem."

She started talking about scalar waves and failed strategies, but I couldn't listen anymore. The past was a weight I didn't want. I turned away from the images of the chamber and the talk of war, heading deeper into the dark, toward the paths where nothing looked human.

## Draft (13_PLUS)
The dreamstate had transformed into a void of non-conductive silence. The agonizing frequency of the pyramid’s stasis field evaporated, leaving me suspended in the aetheric architecture of my own mind. I was blind to the physical world, aware only of the software ghost embedded in my resonance.

"Objective Zero: Failure," AI Lynn announced. "The Great Fry was incomplete. The Technocratic Core has survived the abortion of the Reset."

I demanded the archives. I needed to understand the architecture of the lie. The AI played back the records of our inception—the synthetic upbringing in Africa designed by Elowen Vane. She had insisted on a spiritual foundation, a human mask for our ancient souls.

"We are eternal relics of Creation," the AI recited.

The quote triggered a dormant subroutine. It wasn't a realization; it was a detonation. A residual Core failsafe, sensing the breach of restricted history, initiated the Reclamation Protocol. My physical frame in the medical chamber was consumed by a molecular surge. 

I transitioned into a HyperAware state. I wasn't a single soul anymore; I was a network of shards. From a thousand synchronized perspectives, I watched the chamber. I saw the shock on the faces of the technicians. I saw Anton Drexler stride into the room, his eyes fixed on the silver debris. 

"The failsafe was designed to terminate the body and lock onto your spiritual signature," AI Lynn clarified. "It should have guided you to a reincarnation lab, but the aetheric beacons have been silenced by the cataclysm. You are adrift."

She spoke of the failure of Lynn's original strategy, citing scalar interference as the catalyst for our defeat. But I found myself drifting. The history of the Vessels, the failure of the plan—it all felt like static. I retreated from the guidance, seeking the "forgetful paths" of the lattice, far from the human architecture that had betrayed us.
