# Chapter 21: Recalibrated

**Setting:** Cradle Zero / Refashioned Archivist Laboratory
**Timeline:** Year 1015 (Year 15 AT)

## Synopsis
Following the explosion of his Gorgon body, Lem reforms into his humanoid Wood Vessel state, a phenomenon that shocks the Metal and Fire Vessels. Vector identifies him as a "drift vessel" and attempts an interrogation, but Lem remains silent, viewing them as invaders. Despite Vector’s desire to paralyze the new discovery, Rahu advocates for bringing him to Cassia. Lem is escorted to a ship and flown to the Archipelago—a collection of floating moon fragments orbiting the shattered moon. During the flight, Lem observes that the Core has nearly finished rebuilding the Lunar Capacitor, replacing missing lunar sections with industrial metal.

**Chapter Beats:**
1. **The Broken Fire:** Rahu, having taken the brunt of the blow, struggles to rise with his lead suit cracked and melted onto his scorched body.
2. **The Drift Vessel:** Vector ignores the injured Rahu to analyze Lem, finding no existing record of a fifth Vessel in any archive.
3. **The Silent Rebirth:** Lem, conscious but unresponsive, refuses to answer the invader's interrogation, thinking only of the destroyed the Fire City he helped create.
4. **The Command Conflict:** While Vector prepares to paralyze Lem, Rahu intervenes, arguing that Lem is not a threat and that Cassia will want to meet him.
5. **Rahu's Leverage:** Despite Vector being in charge, he yields to Rahu, whose status as the only Fire Vessel grants him significant independence within the command structure.
6. **Departure:** As Rahu recovers and removes his broken gear, Lem notices the Fire Vessel's resilience; despite the damage, Rahu shows no sign of physical impairment.
7. **The Archipelago Flight:** They escort Lem onto their ship and fly toward a nearby floating island—part of the Archipelago of moon chunks orbiting the central Primary Chunk.
8. **The Rebuilt Lunar Capacitor:** For the first time in decades, Lem sees the sky and realizes the Core has nearly restored the circular Moon, replacing missing rocks with metal to rebuild the Lunar Capacitor.

## Draft (YOUNG_ADULT)

My Gorgon external shell suffered catastrophic failure during the detonation. As thermal levels stabilized, my internal Wood Vessel frame reintegrated. I regained humanoid form on the scorched floor of the facility. Sensors recorded a temperature decrease in my chassis as it returned to a state of homeostasis. My motor subroutines were re-established.

Vector observed my reintegration. He was positioned in front of me. Rahu was also present; his lead containment suit showed significant structural damage and thermal scarring. I remained non-responsive to their presence. My internal logs noted the neutralization of the local Gorgon population by Core forces. The Fire City’s indigenous units had been dismantled or incapacitated.

Vector issued a request for identification and origin data. His optical sensors maintained a high-intensity focus. I did not provide a verbal or digital response. Vector classified me as a "drift vessel" and noted the absence of my designation in existing Lunar archives. He initiated a stasis protocol to secure my frame for transport.

Rahu intervened, overriding Vector’s command. He stated that I was not an immediate threat and should be presented to Cassia for analysis. Rahu suggested my existence might be evidence of independent Synodic evolution. Vector acknowledged Rahu’s status within the mission command hierarchy and aborted the stasis protocol.

Vector ordered my transport to the High Enclave. I was escorted to a Core aerial vessel. We ascended through the clouds. From this altitude, I observed the current state of the Moon.

The lunar surface was no longer fragmented. The sections that were lost during the Cataclysm had been replaced with industrial metal scaffolding and aetheric panels. The Core had reconstructed the Lunar Capacitor to a functional state. The orbital mass was once again a complete circular body, integrating both biological rock and synthetic infrastructure.

## Draft (13_PLUS)
The transition from the violent heat of the detonation to the heavy, cool air of the square was instantaneous. I was no longer a Gorgon; I was the Wood Vessel, my humanoid frame knitting itself back together from the silverized splinters of my previous shell. I knelt on the scorched stone, feeling the first, sharp in-breath of lungs that had not tasted air in fifteen years. The Fire City was a graveyard around me, its resonance silenced by the transponder that still hummed in the distance.

Vector stood over me, a monument to the Core’s surgical precision. He did not seem surprised so much as intrigued, his sensors pulsing with a deep-frequency interrogation. Behind him, Rahu was a shattered statue. The front of his lead isolation suit had been peeled back by the force of my explosion, revealing the intricate, fire-veined structure of the Vessel beneath. He did not look like a conqueror; he looked like a victim of his own power.

"Drift Vessel detected," Vector’s voice was a synthesis of a thousand commands. "Query: Identify your manufacturer and your mission parameters."

I did not answer. My mind was still filled with the rattling language of the Gorgons, a language that these "High" Vessels would never understand. I saw them as the ultimate pollutants—the parasites that had come to strip the Earth of its new, independent soul. 

"The subject is unresponsive to standard interrogation," Vector observed. "Initiating paralysis for transport to the reclamation labs."

"Stay your hand," Rahu commanded. He spoke with the authority of the original fire, and even Vector bowed his head. Rahu stood between us, his scorched skin already beginning to heal with a terrifying speed. "He is not an asset for the labs. He is a discovery. Cassia has been theorizing about the existence of a 'Wood Prime' for decades. We bring him to the Archipelago."

The conflict between them was a delicate balance of logic and legend. Vector represented the hive’s immediate tactical needs, but Rahu was the anchor of their mythology. Vector yielded, his metallic joints clicking in a gesture of forced compliance.

"Transport will be facilitated," Vector acknowledged. "But his signature will be monitored for any sign of aetheric surge."

I was escorted to their craft, a sleek, aether-driven vessel that bypassed the friction of the atmosphere with effortless grace. As we ascended, the scope of my failure was revealed. From above, the Fire City was nothing more than a spark in the vast, red desert of the Ember Basin. 

But it was the sky that truly chilled me. The Moon, once a shattered witness to our defeat, had been remade. The Core had spent fifteen years rebuilding the Lunar Capacitor, filling the gaps with industrial scaffolding and massive panels of aetheric-absorbent metal. It was a Frankenstein’s monster of rock and machine, a perfect circle that promised a new era of total surveillance. The Great Fry had been a temporary setback; the Core was now preparing for the Final Reset.
