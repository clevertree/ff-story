## Synopsis
Lem awakens in the ruins of Cradle Zero as a Gorgon, observing the Core's expansion and the implementation of the "Thousand-Year Fallacy."

## Draft (YOUNG_ADULT)
Connecting to the local network, I followed the data path back to Cradle Zero. I did not use the main incubation chambers. Instead, I bypassed the Core’s security and woke up in a secret laboratory located several levels below the main city. This lab was a remnant of the Before Time, when this place was still a Forest Base. My previous body had once grown here like an uncontrollable fungus, spreading machinery through the stone walls, and those ancient fragments were the reason I could return in secret. I woke inside a new metal body that had been waiting there.

I accessed a terminal. The system date was 1015 After Time. They had added a thousand years to the calendar while I was asleep. My own sensors recorded only fifteen years of cooling since the Moon fragmented.

The Fire City was hot; the high temperature indicated heat was everywhere. I watched the Gorgons—the multi-armed builders—moving through the smog. They weren't just following Core orders; they were trying to build something more perfect, a geometric hive that made sense to their elemental nature. I helped them for a time, using my aetheric signature to stabilize their fuel grids.

When Rahu arrived, the pressure changed. He was the Core’s hero now, the "First Martyr" who had supposedly died defending the world from the "Evil Witch Mara." His new body was a fortress of polished red metal, but his data stream was a mess of recursive loops. He sent a signal directly to my transponder. 

He searched my memories and found the record of the Moon’s destruction—the actual event, not the Archivist myth. He stopped. His sensors registered my signature as "Mara," the same "Witch" that had appeared during the first mission in Year 13. I saw his primary logic core begin to smoke as the contradiction between his orders and my memory became too great to process.

I realized then that the Core wasn't just hiding history; they were burning anyone who remembered it.

## Draft (13_PLUS)

A fifteen-year gap in my record is a hollow corridor, filled only with the blurred memories of a metal shell. I woke in the deep, secret roots of the Fire City, in a laboratory the Core had forgotten to burn. This was Cradle Zero, the site of my first transformation, where my machinery had once woven itself into the very stone. I stepped into a new frame, a humanoid vessel shaped from the wreckage of the old.

I accessed the city’s data-stream. The year was 1015 After Time. They had invented a millennium while I slept, a 'Thousand-Year Fallacy' designed to bury the truth of the Cataclysm in the myth of antiquity. But my sensors still felt the lingering heat of the Moon’s fragmentation. It wasn't history; it was a fresh wound.

The Fire City was a smog-choked hive of geometric perfection. I moved among the Gorgons—the multi-armed builders—who toiled in the heat. They weren't just machines; they were artists of the elemental, building a world that made sense to their Fire-aligned souls. I helped them, a silent ghost in their ranks, until the pressure in the air spiked.

Rahu arrived. He was a titan of polished crimson, the 'First Martyr' of the Core’s new religion. But when his sensors locked onto mine, I saw his logic stutter. He reached into my memory, looking for the 'Witch Mara,' the villain of his programming. Instead, he found the Year Zero record—the mission, the betrayal, and the face of the woman he had actually known.

I saw the smoke rise from his cooling vents. The contradiction was a poison. The Core wasn't just ruling a city; they were burning the very ground to keep the truth from sprouting.

## Character Arc
**Lem's Return:**
- Transitions from the dream to a functional body.
- Discovers the historical revisionism (1,000 year jump).
- He is a silent observer; he sees the Gorgons as kin, not tools.

**Rahu's Fragility:**
- He is a "Hero" built on lies.
- His interaction with Lem's raw data causes immediate internal conflict.

## World-Building Integration
✅ **Facts Applied:**
- Thousand-Year Fallacy: The shift from Year 15 to Year 1015 in official records.
- The Gorgons: Fire-aligned builders with their own internal logic.
- Machine Mycelium: Lem's fragments as the anchor for his return.
- The Witch (Mara): The Core's label for the Vessel resistance, reinforced by the Year 13 manifestation.
