## Synopsis
The "Shore of Shadows"; Arlo rejects the identifier of a Vessel identity.


---

## Draft
The ridge faced a cold sea. Some people called it the Shore of Shadows. Arlo burned his identifier plate. He placed the remains in a box. He said he was not a Vessel.

This action was repeated by others. They did not make public announcements. Many villagers began to remove their tracking beacons and ceased to attend mandatory briefings. The village documents show that several people stopped following their assigned schedules.

These actions required us to change our logistics. The protocols were no longer effective because the villagers did not comply with the established rules. Refusal became a common method for the residents of the ridge.

## Character Arc
**Lem's Architect Era:**
- He moves from "Soldier" to "Creator."
- He accepts the moral weight of building an army of his own "kind."

**Arlo's Resilience:**
- He demonstrates that human will is the only thing the Core cannot simulate.
- His departure creates a vacuum of leadership that Myrr and Lem must fill.

## World-Building Integration
✅ **Facts Applied:**
- Shore of Shadows: The boundary where the Core's signal interference becomes a physical wall of static.
- Hybrid Fabrication: Using Wood Vessel "Blooming" to create Metal/Fire hardware.
- City Nodes: The synchronization points for the "Loom of Time" and civilian monitoring.
- Plan B: The shift from "Vessel Awakening" to "Industrial Sabotage."
- The Fallacy: The lie that the Moon was a natural disaster; revealed as a controlled demolition.

✅ **The Grown Army:**
- The units are semi-organic, self-repairing, and un-hackable by the Core's digital backdoors.
- They are stealth machines that do not appear on standard sensors.
