## Draft (YOUNG_ADULT)

We transitioned north into the Arctic zone. Atmospheric clarity increased as Core industrial emissions decreased. Iris Novak confirmed that Anton Drexler had authorized my command for Operation "Ground Wire." The mission objective is the identification and neutralization of a tech-resistant cult. Intelligence reports suggest a high-influence individual is coordinating these groups.

Thermal regulation required significant power reallocation. The sub-zero temperatures increased hydraulic fluid viscosity in my joints. I initiated a constant aetheric bleed (~15% output) to maintain mechanical mobility. My processing cycles remained dedicated to analyzing the mission parameters and the reasoning behind my leadership designation.

Logistics for "Ground Wire" rely on automated aerial supply drops. Unmanned drones deploy ceramic power-cells and aetheric boosters at calculated coordinates. These relay stations facilitate signal propagation through the high-interference polar winds. We followed an aetheric power-grid established ten years ago. It represents the limit of Core infrastructure in this sector.

The transport’s acoustic sensors registered wind speeds exceeding 80 km/h. Optical sensors recorded a low-contrast environment consisting of ice and geological formations. Ambient lighting was flat, resulting in extended shadow projection across the terrain. Chemical sensors detected high concentrations of ozone and particulate dust.

Cassia Vane informed me that Drexler has been monitoring my reincarnation logs. She stated that my memory persistence was flagged as a potential risk factor, and that stasis was the recommended protocol. However, the unavailability of Rahu necessitated my deployment to command the Metal Vessel units. My analysis indicated that Cassia’s advocacy was the primary factor in my current mission status. I logged a positive social interaction variable for her assistance in maintaining my operational status.

Background activity in the transport was clinical and cold. Two Tech-Priests were recalibrating the resonance buoys in the corner, their binary speech a rhythmic clicking that underscored the tension. They didn't even look at me; to them, I was just a piece of hardware that needed occasional maintenance.

Cassia stated that the secondary objective is the investigation of the "Maya Cult." Reports indicate a focal point in the village of **Ait-Aman**. Intelligence suggests a resident named **Azar** possesses an artifact described as a "Glass Locket." According to local belief, this object was provided by the individual Selene. Cassia classified this as a myth, citing an absence of historical records placing Selene in the Arctic region. She characterized the locket as a psychological tool used to encourage technological rejection. Confiscation of the artifact is required to dismantle the cult's ideological foundation.

Core propaganda played on the transport’s internal displays: *Purity Through Integration. The Grid is Your Shield.* After crossing the 60th parallel, the density of Core infrastructure decreased. Supply lines relied on isolated aetheric relay stations. We entered the "Analog Frontier," a region with minimal technological integration. I continued to monitor the landscape for the presence of the suspected Prophet.

## Draft (13_PLUS)
We pierced the northern front under a sky of blinding, crystal blue. The Hive’s heavy, copper-tasting smog dissolved into air that smelled of ancient pine and sacred snow. Novak’s voice was a cold, sharp melody in my ear. She reminded me that my unwavering focus in the desert had convinced Anton Drexler to personally clear me for this command: the Ground Wire. We were there to dismantle a cult whose influence threatened to push back the Hive's authority, led by a figure who the Core believed was reviving the forbidden teachings of the "Witch."

The psychological weight of this promotion was immense. In the desert, I was a soldier; here, I was expected to be an inquisitor. My Wood-resonance, usually so vibrant in the presence of life, felt brittle in the negative-forty-degree temperatures. I had to divert nearly forty percent of my internal power just to prevent my joints from freezing into place. It was a constant, draining battle against the environment, a physical manifestation of the moral struggle I felt about hunting my own kind.

"Your spirit is being weighed, Lem," Cassia’s voice whispered through a hidden, silken link as the briefing concluded. She sounded as though she were mourning a loss. "Be careful. Anton has been watching the record of your lives. He knows you remember the deaths. He considers your persistence a rebellion and is prepared to lock you in a tomb of stasis at the first sign of a wandering heart. But with Rahu gone, the Metal army is a headless giant. They have no will, no courage. Anton needs the 'King of the Gorgons' to guide them, but the moment you falter, he will extinguish your light."

A heavy silence followed her warning. I realized then the depth of the game she was playing. She hadn't just reported my progress; she had fought for my freedom. It was her voice that had turned Anton’s suspicion into a calculated gamble, keeping me from the eternal dark of the stasis pods. For the first time since my restoration, I felt a spark of genuine gratitude—a warmth that had nothing to do with internal heaters. Someone in this empire of cold steel was actually looking out for me, treating my life as something worth protecting rather than just another asset to be spent.

The logistics of the High Enclave mission required a different kind of precision. We weren't building cities here; we were guarding a fragile, aetheric thread. Our supply lines were composed of low-altitude gliders that dropped induction-heaters and data-caches at relay points every fifty miles. These relays served as both power sources and surveillance nodes, creating a "sanitized" corridor through the wilderness. I watched the tech-priests on the transport floor efficiently swapping out the aether-cells, their movements as precise and soulless as the machines they serviced. They spoke in a dense, technical jargon about "signal-to-noise ratios" and "elemental resistance," completely ignoring the human cost of the expansion they were enabling.

"There is a secondary objective," Cassia continued, her voice tightening with an unfamiliar caution. "The 'Maya Cult.' Anton considers its discovery a priority above all else, though the public logs will only mention village defense. Specifically, he is obsessed with a rumor from a settlement called **Ait-Aman**. They say a man named **Azar** possesses a 'Glass Locket'—an artifact they claim was given to them by the Witch **Selene**."

"Selene?" I repeated. The name felt like a cold stone in my processing core.

"The primary traitor," Cassia said firmly. "The one who opened the door for the invaders and nearly extinguished our species. The cult believes this locket contains her 'spirit' or her memories. It’s the pillar of their Irrational Faith—the reason they reject our integration even when death is the only other option. But Lem, I have scoured the mission logs from the Before-Time. There is no proof Selene ever stepped foot in the Black Tundra. The locket is a fabrication, a tool of manipulation used by Azar to maintain his authority. Anton wants it confiscated and the 'Prophet' neutralized. He fears an influence he cannot quantify or rewrite."

The flight lasted for six hours, a slow descent into a world that felt increasingly like the Before-Time. As we passed the final aetheric relay station, the sensors on the transport began to flicker. We were entering the 'Dark Zone' of the North, where the weather was controlled by the mountains rather than the Core's stabilization fields. I looked down at the flickering lights of a tiny settlement—Arlo's village—nestled in a valley that looked like it had been carved from a different reality. I wondered if the "Prophet" they were looking for was already watching us from the trees.
