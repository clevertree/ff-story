# Chapter 29: The Tripod Anomaly

**Timeline:** Year 15 AT
**POV:** Lem
**Setting:** Northern Forest - Engagement Zone

---

## Outline / Summary

The Core is unhappy with the peaceful conclusion in Lem's region. They order Lem and his entire crew to hunt down the Tripods that bypassed the village. The mission jumps from easy to complex as the soldiers plan a deep forest incursion.

Lem doesn't see why this mission is necessary anymore. He watches as the soldiers spread out with vehicles, tracking the Tripods like animals. Eventually, they corner the machines. The Tripods defend themselves violently, even using a makeshift giant shield made of scrap metal to protect themselves from the array of heavy Core weaponry. But it isn't enough; the machines are destroyed.

A small regiment of Metal Vessels is dispatched to find the crash-landed "pod" and destroy it, while Lem is ordered back to the village. The "threat" is declared eliminated.

Returning to the village, Lem attempts to debrief the survivors. The villagers are unanimous: the trouble was brought here by the Corps' beacons. They demand the removal of the tracking devices, convinced the Tripods wouldn't have bothered them if not for the soldiers' presence. The Core officers try to show records of the saved villages in Rahu's region, but the villagers refuse to look at "more technology."

Before leaving, the Core makes a final offer of conscription to **Arlo**. The boy is torn; his love for technology pulls him toward the Corps, but the rigidity of his people and the suspicious actions of the soldiers hold him back. He refuses, choosing to stay in the village he has never left.

## Character Arc

### Lem
- **Emotional/Psychological State:** Skeptical and weary. He feels the "grinding" nature of the mission cycle—throwing Vessels at objectives until they are met.
- **Key Beats:**
    1. Witnessing the "violent" destruction of the non-aggressive Tripods.
    2. Realizing the villagers' resentment is justified; the Core consolidates instead of protects.
    3. The return to the desert base for a debriefing that will challenge Cassia Vane.

### Arlo
- **Emotional/Psychological State:** Heartbroken and conflicted. He survives the encounter but is left in the "dirt" he loves, watching the high-tech saviors leave with their beacons still active.
    2. Witnessing the Metal Vessel instigate the slaughter.
    3. Hearing Arlo's refusal of recruitment; it gives him the courage to question his own orders.

### Arlo
- **Key Beat:** The Refusal. He rejects the Archivists' offer of a "better life," exposing the moral void of their utopia.

### Cassia Vane
- **Status:** Shaken. She tries to justify the Metal Vessel's shot as "pre-emptive protection," but her voice wavers. She informs Lem he is "excused" from the remaining phases of Mission 2.
